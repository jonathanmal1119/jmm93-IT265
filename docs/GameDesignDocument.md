# Over 30

## High Level

**Over 30** is a strategic card game where players aim to reach a total as close to 30 as possible without exceeding it. Players draw from a custom deck consisting of positive and negative number cards, along with powerful effect cards that can alter totals in unique ways.

At the start of each round, every player receives one effect card, which they can choose to play on themselves or an opponent to influence the game. In addition, each round, a dealer plays a global effect card that impacts all players who are still active in the round.

Players who choose to stand are locked into their total and are no longer affected by future cards or effects. This creates a balance between risk and timing, as players must decide when to keep playing and when to secure their score.

# Change Log

* The title was changed to **Over 30** to better reflect the values players were getting in playtesting.
* Removed many negative effects from effect cards because player sums were fluctuating too heavily (e.g. `-10 → 10`). The goal became encouraging players to steadily build toward high positive values.
* Changed the card range from the original `2 → 10` to `-10 → 10`, excluding `0` and `1`.
* Implemented `-1`, `0`, and `1` cards, but they did not impact gameplay enough, so more positive cards were added instead.
* Changed the drawing system to random to prevent card counting.
* Focused more heavily on gameplay systems instead of deep story/background elements due to time and project scope constraints.

# Gameplay Pillars

## Risk Creates Excitement

Every decision should feel strategic and meaningful, forcing players to choose between safety and greed.

## Chaos Changes Everything

Dealer effects and player effect cards should constantly shift the game state and prevent predictable outcomes.

## Easy to Learn, Hard to Master

The core rules should be simple to understand while still allowing for deep strategy.

## Fast and Competitive

Rounds should move quickly to keep players engaged and encourage repeated play sessions.

---

# Unique Selling Points

## Positive and Negative Number System

Using both positive and negative number cards is uncommon in this style of card game. This creates unique strategies and differentiates the gameplay.

## Player Sabotage Mechanics

Each player receives an effect card every round, allowing them to sabotage opponents or strengthen their own position.

## Stand Protection Mechanic

Standing locks in a player's total and protects them from future effects, creating tension around when to stop drawing cards.

## Fast-Paced Replayability

Short rounds combined with randomized effects and card draws ensure matches feel different every time.

---

# Visual Design

## Minimalist Interface

The UI focuses on simplicity by displaying:

* Current player hand
* Player effect card
* Dealer cards
* Current total

This keeps gameplay readable and easy to follow during fast-paced matches.
